<tr><td align=center><h1 style='color:#1A5CC8'>Common Polynomial</h1><font><b><span style='font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green'>Time Limit: 3000/1000 MS (Java/Others)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Memory Limit: 32768/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 101&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Accepted Submission(s): 25<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Problem Description</div> <div class=panel_content>Math teacher Mr. Matsudaira is teaching expansion and factoring of polynomials to his students.<br><br>Last week he instructed the students to write two polynomials (with a single variable x), and to report GCM (greatest common measure) of them as a homework, but he found it boring to check their answers manually. So you are asked to write a program to check the answers.<br><br>Hereinafter, only those polynomials with integral coefficients, called integral polynomials, are considered.<br><br>When two integral polynomials A and B are given, an integral polynomial C is a common factor of A and B if there are some integral polynomials X and Y such that A = CX and B = CY .<br><br>GCM of two integral polynomials is a common factor which has the highest degree (for x, here); you have to write a program which calculates the GCM of two polynomials. It is known that GCM of given two polynomials is unique when constant multiplication factor is ignored. That is, when C and D are both GCM of some two polynomials A and B, p×C = q×D for some nonzero integers p and q.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Input</div> <div class=panel_content>The input consists of multiple datasets. Each dataset constitutes a pair of input lines, each representing a polynomial as an expression defined below.<br><br>1. A primary is a variable x, a sequence of digits 0 – 9, or an expression enclosed within (· · · ). Examples: x, 99, (x+1).<br><br>2. A factor is a primary by itself or a primary followed by an exponent. An exponent consists of a symbol ^ followed by a sequence of digits 0 – 9. Examples: x^05, 1^15, (x+1)^3.<br>3. A term consists of one or more adjoining factors. Examples: 4x, (x+1)(x-2), 3(x+1)^2.<br><br>4. An expression is one or more terms connected by either + or -. Additionally, the first term of an expression may optionally be preceded with a minus sign -. Examples: -x+1,3(x+1)^2-x(x-1)^2.<br>Integer constants, exponents, multiplications (adjoining), additions (+) and subtractions/negations (-) have their ordinary meanings. A sequence of digits is always interpreted as an integer constant. <br><br>For example, 99 means 99, not 9 × 9.<br><br>Any subexpressions of the input, when fully expanded normalized, have coefficients less than 100 and degrees of x less than 10. Digit sequences in exponents represent non-zero values. All the datasets are designed so that a standard algorithm with 32-bit two’s complement integers can solve the problem without overflows.<br><br>The end of the input is indicated by a line containing a period.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Output</div> <div class=panel_content>For each of the dataset, output GCM polynomial expression in a line, in the format below. c0x^p0 ± c1x^p1 · · · ± cnx^pn<br><br>Where ci and pi (i = 0, . . . , n) are positive integers with p0 &gt; p1 &gt; · · · &gt; pn, and the greatest common divisor of {ci | i = 0; · · · ; n} is 1.<br><br>Additionally:<br><br>1. When ci is equal to 1, it should be omitted unless corresponding pi is 0,<br>2. x^0 should be omitted as a whole, and<br>3. x^1 should be written as x.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Input</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">-(x^3-3x^2+3x-1)
(x-1)^2
x^2+10x+25
x^2+6x+5
x^3+1
x-1
.</div></pre></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Output</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">x^2-2x+1
x+5
1</div></pre></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Source</div> <div class=panel_content> <a href="/search.php?field=problem&key=2008+Asia+Regional+Aizu&source=1&searchmode=source"> 2008 Asia Regional Aizu </a> </div> <div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Recommend</div> <div class=panel_content>lcy</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><center style='font-size:15px;font-family:Arial;font-weight:bold;color:#1A5CC8'><a href='statistic.php?pid=2916'>Statistic</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;<a href='submit.php?pid=2916'>Submit</a>&nbsp;|&nbsp;<a href="./discuss/problem/list.php?problemid=2916">Discuss</a> | <a href='note/note.php?pid=2916'>Note</a><br></td></tr><tr>
