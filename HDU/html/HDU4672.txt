<tr><td align=center><h1 style='color:#1A5CC8'>Present Day, Present Time</h1><font><b><span style='font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green'>Time Limit: 3000/1500 MS (Java/Others)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Memory Limit: 65535/65535 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 553&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Accepted Submission(s): 229<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Problem Description</div> <div class=panel_content>Sangxin and Bicycle play games everyday, but no matter what they play, Sangxin always loses. Sangxin found that there is no chance to beat Bicycle even playing dice.<br>One day, Sangxin came up with a brand new game with stones. In this game there are n piles of stones and m recycling centers, each pile of stones has A<sub>i</sub> stones and each recycling center could process <strong>exactly</strong> B<sub>i</sub> (which is pre-assigned) stones at a time. Sangxin and Bicycle operate by turns. Each turn the player must pick one piles of stones and recycle some stones in that pile. Note that they can use recycling centers unlimited times in one turn. The one couldn't do anything during his turn loses.<br>After thinking a while Sangxin thought the game is too easy and appended a weird rule: <strong>After</strong> someone's turn if there are no way to completely recycle any pile of stones, he loses immediately.<br>Now, given the initial state of the game, Sangxin wants to know that who will win. Assume they will play in optimal strategy. The players' name and who will play first is given.<br></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Input</div> <div class=panel_content>The input contains several test cases, terminated by EOF. Most of test cases are rather small.<br>Each case has 4 lines, the first line contains two integer n (1 ≤ n ≤ 100000) and m (1 ≤ m ≤ 100), which denotes the number of stone piles and recycling centers. The second line contains two string (length not greater than 20 and contains only lowercase letters) indicating the players' name. The first player in that line plays first. The third line contains n integers, i-th denotes A<sub>i</sub> (1 ≤ A<sub>i</sub> ≤ 1000000000) and next line contains m integer, i-th denotes B<sub>i</sub>(1 ≤ B<sub>i</sub> ≤ 100).<br></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Output</div> <div class=panel_content>For each case output the winner's name on separate lines.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Input</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">3 2
sangxin bike
1 2 3
2 3
3 1
bike sangxin
3 4 9
1</div></pre></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Output</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">bike
