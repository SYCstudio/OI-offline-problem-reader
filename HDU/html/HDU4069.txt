<tr><td align=center><h1 style='color:#1A5CC8'>Squiggly Sudoku</h1><font><b><span style='font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green'>Time Limit: 4000/2000 MS (Java/Others)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Memory Limit: 65536/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 2008&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Accepted Submission(s): 837<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Problem Description</div> <div class=panel_content>Today we play a squiggly <a href=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudoku>sudoku</a>, The objective is to fill a 9*9 grid with digits so that each column, each row, and each of the nine Connecting-sub-grids that compose the grid  contains all of the digits from 1 to 9.<br>Left figure is the puzzle and right figure is one solution.<br><center><img style='max-width:100%;' src=../../data/images/C348-1009-1.jpg></center> <br>Now, give you the information of the puzzle, please tell me is there no solution or multiple solution or one solution.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Input</div> <div class=panel_content>The first line is a number T(1&lt;=T&lt;=2500), represents the number of case.
