<tr><td align=center><h1 style='color:#1A5CC8'>Liang Guo Sha</h1><font><b><span style='font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green'>Time Limit: 2000/1000 MS (Java/Others)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Memory Limit: 32768/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 808&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Accepted Submission(s): 603<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Problem Description</div> <div class=panel_content>Maybe you know “San Guo Sha”, but I guess you didn’t hear the game: “Liang Guo Sha”!<br><br>Let me introduce this game to you. Unlike “San Guo Sha” with its complicated rules, “Liang Guo Sha” is a simple game, it consists only four cards, two cards named “Sha”, and the other named “Shan”. <br><br>Alice and Bob are good friends, and they’re playing “Liang Guo Sha” now. <font color=red>Everyone has two cards: a “Sha” and a “Shan”. </font> Each round, everyone choose a card of his/her own, and show it together(Just show the selected card, do not need to put it away). If both of them choose “Sha”,<b> then Alice gets A points</b>, <font color=red>and Bob loses A points</font>; if both of them choose “Shan”, <b>then Alice gets B points</b>,<font color=red> and Bob loses B points</font>; otherwise, <b>Bob gets C points</b>, <font color=red>and Alice loses C points</font>.&nbsp;&nbsp;<br><br><font color=red>Both Alice and Bob wants to get points as many as possible, they thought a optimal strategy: Calculating a percentage of choosing card “Sha” in order to ensure that even the opponent uses the optimal strategy, he/she can still get a highest point exceptation.</font><br>&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>Here is the question, if both Alice and Bob use the optimal strategy to make their points higher, what is the expectation point which Alice can get in a round?</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Input</div> <div class=panel_content>Several test case, process to EOF.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;Each test case has only a line, consists three positive integers: A, B, C respectively.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;1 &lt;= A, B, C &lt;= 100000</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Output</div> <div class=panel_content>Each test case just need to output one line, the expectation point that Alice can get. Round to 6 decimal points.</div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Input</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">2 10 4
3 3 3</div></pre></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Output</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">0.200000
0.000000
<div style='font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:14px;background-color:F4FBFF;border:#B7CBFF 1px dashed;padding:6px'><div style='font-family:Arial;font-weight:bold;color:#7CA9ED;border-bottom:#B7CBFF 1px dashed'><i>Hint</i></div>
In test case 1, both Alice and Bob calculated the best percentage of choosing “Sha”, and the their percentage are the same: 70%.
If Bob do not choose the best percentage, his strategy might be targetd.

For example, if Bob choose 100%, then Alice can change her percentage to 100%, Bob might lose many points. Bob is clever, so he won’t do that.
