<tr><td align=center><h1 style='color:#1A5CC8'>Fishhead’s Little Game</h1><font><b><span style='font-family:Arial;font-size:12px;font-weight:bold;color:green'>Time Limit: 2000/1000 MS (Java/Others)&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Memory Limit: 32768/32768 K (Java/Others)<br>Total Submission(s): 1267&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Accepted Submission(s): 388<br></span></b></font><br><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Problem Description</div> <div class=panel_content>　　　There is a 3 by 3 grid and each vertex is assigned a number.<br><br><center><img style='max-width:100%;' src=../../data/images/C479-1006-1.jpg></center> <br>　　　It looks like JiuGongGe, but they are different, for we are not going to fill the cell but the edge. For instance,<br><br><center><img style='max-width:100%;' src=../../data/images/C479-1006-2.jpg></center> <br><center> adding edge 6 –&gt; 10</center> <br>　　　The rule of this game is that each player takes turns to add an edge. You will get one point if the edge you just added, together with edges already added before, forms a new square (only square of size 1 is considered). Of course, you get two points if that edge forms two squares. Notice that an edge can be added only once.<br><br><center><img style='max-width:100%;' src=../../data/images/C479-1006-3.jpg></center> <br><center>forming two squares to get two points</center><br>　　Tom200 and Jerry404 is playing this little game, and have played n rounds when Fishhead comes in. Fishhead wants to know who will be the winner. Can you help him? Assume that Tom200 and Jerry404 are clever enough to make optimal decisions in each round. Every Game starts from Tom200.<br></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Input</div> <div class=panel_content>　　The first line of the input contains a single integer T (T &lt;= 100), the number of test cases.<br>　　For each case, the first line contains an integers n (12 &lt;= n &lt;= 24), which means they have taken total n rounds in turn. Next n lines each contains two integers a, b (a, b &lt;= 16) representing the two endpoints of the edge.<br></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Output</div> <div class=panel_content>　　For each case output one line &quot;Case #X: &quot;, representing the Xth case, starting from 1. If Tom200 wins, print &quot;Tom200&quot; on one line, print &quot;Jerry404&quot; otherwise.<br></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Input</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">1
15
1 2
1 5
2 6
5 9
6 10
9 10
5 6
2 3
3 7
7 11
10 11
3 4
6 7
7 8
4 8</div></pre></div><div class=panel_bottom>&nbsp;</div><br><div class=panel_title align=left>Sample Output</div><div class=panel_content><pre><div style="font-family:Courier New,Courier,monospace;">Case #1: Tom200
<div style='font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:14px;background-color:F4FBFF;border:#B7CBFF 1px dashed;padding:6px'><div style='font-family:Arial;font-weight:bold;color:#7CA9ED;border-bottom:#B7CBFF 1px dashed'><i>Hint</i></div>
　　In case 1, Tom200 gets two points when she add edge 5 -> 6, two points in edge 6 -> 7, one point in 4 -> 8.
